# DEX SCORE Requirements

## What Is SCORE?

SCORE (Short-form Client Outcomes for Reporting) is the mandatory outcome measurement framework used in DEX reporting. It measures whether DSS-funded services are making a positive difference for clients across three life domains.

SCORE data is collected from clients at the beginning and end of each 6-month reporting period. It is submitted as part of your DEX data to DSS.

## The Three SCORE Domains

Every client receiving funded services must be measured on all three domains:

**1. Circumstances**
Measures the client's living situation, safety, financial stability, and overall life circumstances. This captures whether their broader situation is improving, staying the same, or deteriorating.

**2. Goals**
Measures the client's progress toward the personal goals they have identified with their service provider. Are they moving closer to what they want to achieve?

**3. Satisfaction**
Measures the client's satisfaction with the service they are receiving. Are they happy with how the support is being delivered?

## The Rating Scale

Each domain is rated on a scale of 1 to 5:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Much worse |
| 2 | A little worse |
| 3 | About the same |
| 4 | A little better |
| 5 | Much better |

At the start of a reporting period, the rating reflects the client's current situation as a baseline. At the end of the period, the rating reflects how things have changed relative to that baseline.

## When to Collect SCORE

SCORE is collected **twice per reporting period** — not at every session:

- **Start of period assessment:** Conducted at or near the first service session of the reporting period (January for Period 1, July for Period 2)
- **End of period assessment:** Conducted at or near the last service session of the reporting period (June for Period 1, December for Period 2)

Trying to collect SCORE at every session is unnecessary and creates worker burden. A brief check-in conversation at the start and end of the period is sufficient.

## The 95% Completeness Requirement

DSS requires that at least **95% of active clients** have complete SCORE data — both start-of-period and end-of-period ratings across all three domains — for each reporting period.

Falling below 95% will trigger a compliance query from your DSS funding manager. Consistently low completeness rates can affect your funding assessment.

With a large client base, 95% means you can afford very few gaps. A provider with 100 active clients can miss no more than 5 before triggering a query.

## Who Collects SCORE

SCORE is typically collected by the support worker or case manager who has the closest relationship with the client. It is a brief verbal check-in conversation — it does not require clinical training or a formal assessment process.

**Suggested approach:**
"At the start of our time together this period, I just want to check in on a few things. Compared to 6 months ago, how are things going with your living situation — better, worse, or about the same? And what about your goals — are you making progress? And are you happy with the support you're getting from us?"

The worker records the responses (1–5 scale for each domain) immediately after the conversation.

## Common SCORE Failures

**Missing end-of-period assessments**
The most common failure. Workers collect start-of-period SCORE but forget or do not have the opportunity to collect end-of-period when clients are discharged, hospitalised, or simply not seen at the right time.

**Collecting only one domain**
All three domains (Circumstances, Goals, Satisfaction) must be collected. Some workers only ask about satisfaction because it feels most natural, leaving the other two incomplete.

**Not collecting for new clients mid-period**
Clients who start receiving services partway through a reporting period still need SCORE collected. The start-of-period assessment happens at their first session.

**Relying on workers to remember**
Without a system prompt, SCORE collection depends entirely on worker memory. This is unreliable at scale.

## SCORE and DEX Submission

When you submit your DEX XML file, the SCORE data for each client is included as part of their client record. Each client must have:
- A valid SLK
- Session records for the period
- Start-of-period SCORE (all 3 domains)
- End-of-period SCORE (all 3 domains)

Missing SCORE data for a client will show as incomplete in the DEX validation report.

## How SupportIQ Manages SCORE

SupportIQ prompts support workers to collect SCORE data through the caregiver mobile app at the start and end of each reporting period. Workers see a notification when a client is due for SCORE collection. The three-domain rating is captured in the app in under 60 seconds. SupportIQ tracks completeness in real time on the compliance dashboard — providers can see their current completeness percentage at any time and identify which clients still need SCORE collected before the period closes.
