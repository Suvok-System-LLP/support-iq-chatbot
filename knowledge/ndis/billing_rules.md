# NDIS Billing Rules

## How NDIS Billing Works

When an NDIS service provider delivers a support to a participant, they are entitled to claim payment from the participant's NDIS plan. The process works like this:

1. Deliver the support (complete the shift or session)
2. Record the shift details (participant, date, support type, hours, worker)
3. Create a payment request with the correct support item code and rate
4. Submit the payment request through PRODA (the government claims portal)
5. NDIA processes the claim — usually within 3 business days
6. Payment is deposited into the provider's nominated bank account

Getting the details right at step 3 is where most errors occur.

## The Three Funding Categories

Every NDIS participant's plan is divided into funding categories. You can only claim for a support under the category it belongs to.

**Core Supports**
The largest category for most participants. Covers everyday supports including assistance with daily activities, community participation, transport, and consumables. Most NDIS support worker shifts are billed under Core Supports.

**Capacity Building Supports**
Covers supports designed to build the participant's independence and skills over time. Includes therapy, support coordination, employment support, and social skills development. Capacity Building has sub-categories and the funding in each sub-category can only be used for that type of support.

**Capital Supports**
One-off or major purchases including assistive technology (wheelchairs, communication devices) and home modifications. Capital funding cannot be used for support worker hours.

## Support Item Codes

Every support you deliver has a specific support item code from the NDIS Support Catalogue. The code tells the NDIA exactly what type of support was delivered. Using the wrong code — even if the rate is correct — will result in claim rejection.

Support item codes follow this structure:
**Registration Group _ Support Purpose _ Support Category _ Support Type _ Indicator**

Example: `01_002_0107_1_1` — Assistance with Self-Care Activities, weekday daytime rate.

## Price Guide Caps

The NDIS Price Guide sets the maximum hourly rate for every support item. You cannot charge above the cap. If your payment request exceeds the cap, it will be automatically rejected. The price guide is updated annually and sometimes more frequently for specific items.

Your NDIS margin is the difference between what you can claim (price guide rate) and what you must pay your worker (SCHADS rate plus on-costs).

## Same-Day Multiple Shifts

If you deliver multiple shifts to the same participant on the same day, each shift must be billed separately with the correct time-based rate for each. A morning weekday shift and an afternoon weekday shift are both billed at the standard rate. A Sunday morning shift and Sunday afternoon shift both attract the Sunday rate.

## Travel Billing

**Client-related travel** (transporting the participant in a vehicle) can be billed under specific transport support items.

**Worker travel to a client** is handled differently — some providers include it in the hourly rate, others bill it separately under provider travel provisions. The rules are specific and changed in 2023. Check the current Support Catalogue for the correct approach.

## Short Notice Cancellations

If a participant or their representative cancels a support with less than 2 clear business days notice, the provider can claim up to 90% of the agreed support rate for that session. This is called a short notice cancellation claim. Providers must have a written cancellation policy that participants have agreed to before they can use this provision.

## Common Rejection Reasons

- **Plan not current:** The service date falls outside the participant's plan dates
- **Wrong support category:** Claiming a Capacity Building item under Core Supports
- **Rate exceeds cap:** Charging above the current price guide maximum
- **Provider not registered:** Billing for a support type your organisation is not registered to deliver
- **Invalid item code:** Using an outdated or incorrect support item code
- **Duplicate claim:** Attempting to claim for a session already paid

## How SupportIQ Prevents Billing Errors

SupportIQ validates every payment request against the current NDIS Support Catalogue and price guide before submission. It checks the participant's plan dates, verifies the support item code is valid for the registration group, and confirms the rate does not exceed the cap. Claims that fail validation are flagged for review before they reach PRODA — preventing rejections and the admin burden of resubmission.
