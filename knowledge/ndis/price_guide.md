# NDIS Price Guide

## What Is the NDIS Price Guide?

The NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits (commonly called the Price Guide) is the official document that sets the maximum rates NDIS registered providers can charge for each type of support. It is published by the NDIA (National Disability Insurance Agency) and applies to all registered providers across Australia.

The Price Guide does not tell you what you must charge — you can charge less. But you cannot charge more. Any payment request above the applicable price limit will be automatically rejected by the NDIA system.

## How Often It Updates

The Price Guide is reviewed at least annually, typically with changes taking effect on 1 July each year aligned with Fair Work wage increases. Interim updates can occur during the year if the NDIA adjusts specific support items. Providers must always use the current rates — claiming at outdated rates, even if they are lower, can cause processing errors.

## Key Support Item Codes for NDIS Providers

The following are the most commonly used support items for community and home-based NDIS support delivery. Rates shown are approximate 2024–25 figures.

**Assistance with Daily Life — Self-Care Activities**
- Weekday daytime: ~$67.56 per hour
- Weekday evening (after 8pm, before midnight): ~$74.32 per hour
- Weekday night (midnight to 6am): ~$82.45 per hour
- Saturday: ~$94.22 per hour
- Sunday: ~$107.56 per hour
- Public holiday: ~$134.45 per hour

**Assistance with Social, Economic and Community Participation**
- Weekday daytime: ~$67.56 per hour
- Saturday: ~$94.22 per hour
- Sunday: ~$107.56 per hour

**Support Coordination (Capacity Building)**
- Standard rate: ~$100.14 per hour

**Therapeutic Supports — Psychology, Occupational Therapy, Physiotherapy**
- Varies by discipline: ~$193.99 to $214.41 per hour

## How Support Item Codes Work

Support item codes follow a structured format that tells you exactly what the item covers:

`01_002_0107_1_1`

- **01** — Registration Group (Assistance with Daily Life)
- **002** — Item number within the group
- **0107** — Support Purpose (Daily Activities)
- **1** — Support Category (Core Supports)
- **1** — Indicator

Different time-based rates for the same type of support have different item codes — a weekday daytime shift uses a different code to a Sunday shift even though both are delivering the same service.

## Your Margin: Price Guide vs SCHADS

Your financial sustainability as an NDIS provider depends on the gap between what the NDIS price guide allows you to claim and what you must pay your workers under the SCHADS Award.

**Example — Sunday shift:**
- NDIS price guide rate you can claim: ~$107.56/hr
- SCHADS cost for a casual Level 3 worker on Sunday: ~$67.76/hr
- Gross margin before other on-costs: ~$39.80/hr

That margin must cover your overheads, management costs, insurance, and profit. Understanding this equation for every shift type — and managing your rostering accordingly — is essential for financial viability.

## What Happens If You Charge Above the Cap

Payment requests above the current price limit are automatically rejected by the NDIA claims system. The rejection does not trigger any penalty for a first occurrence, but patterns of above-cap billing can attract compliance attention. You will need to resubmit at the correct rate.

## How SupportIQ Keeps Rates Current

SupportIQ's price guide engine is updated whenever the NDIA publishes new rates. Providers do not need to manually update anything. When a payment request is created, the system automatically retrieves the current applicable rate for the support item, date, and time, and uses that rate in the claim. If a rate has changed since the last update, the system flags any shifts that may be affected.
