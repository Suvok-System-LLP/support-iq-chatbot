
# SCHADS Award — Overview

## What Is the SCHADS Award?

The Social, Community, Home Care and Disability Services Industry Award 2010 (SCHADS Award) is the legal minimum employment standard for workers in the community services and disability sector in Australia. It is set by the Fair Work Commission and applies to all NDIS service providers who employ support workers, case managers, and community workers.

If you employ anyone to deliver NDIS supports, this award covers them. Getting it wrong means Fair Work liability — underpayment claims, back-pay orders, and potential civil penalties.

## Who It Covers

- Support workers and disability support workers
- Home care workers and personal care workers
- Case managers and support coordinators
- Community development workers
- Crisis accommodation workers
- Family support workers

## The Three Streams

The SCHADS Award has three classification streams. Each covers different types of work:

**Social and Community Services (SACS)**
Covers disability support, case management, community services, and family services. Most NDIS support workers fall here.

**Home Care**
Covers workers who provide personal care and domestic assistance in a client's home. Relevant for in-home NDIS supports.

**Crisis Accommodation**
Covers workers in crisis shelters and supported accommodation facilities.

## Classification Levels

Workers are classified into Levels 1 through 8 based on their qualifications, experience, and the complexity of work they perform.

- **Level 1–2:** Entry level, no formal qualifications required, works under direct supervision
- **Level 3–4:** Certificate III or IV in Disability or Individual Support, works with some independence
- **Level 5–6:** Diploma qualified or significant experience, may supervise others
- **Level 7–8:** Degree qualified, senior practitioners, team leaders, specialist roles

Getting classification right matters. A worker incorrectly classified at Level 1 when their role and qualifications place them at Level 3 creates an underpayment liability from day one.

## Ordinary Hours

- Standard week: 38 ordinary hours
- Span of hours Monday to Saturday: 6:00am to 10:00pm
- Span of hours Sunday: 8:00am to 10:00pm
- Shifts outside these spans attract penalty rates

## Minimum Engagement

Casual and part-time workers must be paid for a minimum of 2 hours per shift, even if the actual shift is shorter. This is one of the most commonly missed obligations for NDIS providers running short community access shifts.

## Why This Matters for NDIS Providers

The gap between what the NDIS price guide allows you to claim and what you must pay your workers under SCHADS is your margin. Understanding both sides of that equation is essential for financial sustainability.

Providers using manual spreadsheets or generic payroll software frequently make errors — wrong classification, missed penalty rates, forgotten allowances. These errors accumulate quickly across a large workforce.
