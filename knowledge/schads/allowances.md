# SCHADS Award — Allowances

## What Are Allowances?

Allowances are additional payments made to workers on top of their ordinary and penalty rate pay. They compensate workers for specific conditions, expenses, or requirements of their role. Under the SCHADS Award, several allowances are mandatory in the right circumstances — they are not optional extras.

Missed allowances are one of the most common findings in Fair Work audits of NDIS providers.

## Travel Allowance

When a worker uses their own vehicle to travel for work purposes (driving to a client's home, driving between clients), they must be reimbursed at the Award rate of approximately **99 cents per kilometre**.

**What counts as work travel:**
- Driving from one client's home to another client's home
- Driving to a service location at the employer's direction
- Driving to collect supplies or equipment for a client

**What does not count:**
- Travel from the worker's home to their first client (ordinary commute)
- Travel from their last client back home

For providers with large field workforces, travel allowance costs add up significantly. Tracking kilometres accurately — and paying the correct amount — is both a compliance requirement and a budget management necessity.

## Broken Shift Allowance

Approximately **$24.09 per broken shift** when a worker's shift is interrupted by an unpaid break of 2 hours or more. This is separate from and in addition to ordinary pay.

This most commonly occurs with morning/afternoon split rosters in home care. If you regularly roster workers for a 7am–9am shift and a 4pm–6pm shift on the same day, every one of those days triggers the broken shift allowance.

## First Aid Allowance

Approximately **$15.03 per week** for workers who:
1. Hold a current first aid certificate, AND
2. Are required by their employer to use that certificate as part of their role

Both conditions must be met. Simply holding a certificate does not trigger the allowance — the employer must actually designate the worker as the first aid officer for a site, shift, or service.

## Telephone Allowance

When a worker is required to maintain a personal phone and be contactable for on-call or work purposes, they are entitled to reimbursement of the rental/line charges associated with that requirement. This applies to on-call workers who must keep their phone available during standby periods.

## Overnight Away From Home

When a worker is required by their employer to stay away from home overnight for work, the employer must reimburse reasonable costs of accommodation and meals. This applies to workers who travel with clients, attend residential programs in other locations, or otherwise stay away from home at the direction of the employer.

## Protective Clothing

When a worker is required to wear rubber gloves, special protective clothing, or safety equipment to perform their role, the employer must provide the equipment or reimburse the worker for the cost of purchasing it.

## Clothing Repair and Replacement

If a worker's personal clothing (not a uniform) is damaged, soiled, or destroyed in the course of performing their duties, the employer must reimburse the reasonable cost of repair or replacement with a comparable item.

## Who Is Responsible

The employer (the NDIS service provider) is responsible for tracking when allowances apply and paying them. It is not the worker's responsibility to claim them — if the conditions are met, the allowance must be paid.

## Audit Risk

Allowances are consistently flagged in Fair Work investigations and NDIS audit findings. The most common issues are:

- Travel allowance not paid or paid at below the Award rate
- Broken shift allowance missed on split-roster arrangements
- First aid allowance paid to all first-aid-trained staff instead of only those designated to use it
- Telephone allowance not paid to on-call workers

## How SupportIQ Tracks Allowances

SupportIQ captures all the data needed to calculate allowances automatically. Travel distances are logged via GPS on the caregiver app. Broken shifts are identified from shift start/end times. First aid designations are stored in the worker profile. At payroll run, all applicable allowances are calculated and included in the payslip with a clear breakdown.
