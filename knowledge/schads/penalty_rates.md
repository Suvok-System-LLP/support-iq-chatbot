# SCHADS Award — Penalty Rates

## What Are Penalty Rates?

Penalty rates are additional pay that must be applied when workers work outside standard weekday hours — on weekends, public holidays, or in overtime. They are not optional and cannot be waived by agreement with the worker (unless a formal averaging arrangement is in place).

## Standard Penalty Rates

| When | Rate |
|------|------|
| Monday to Friday, ordinary hours (6am–10pm) | 100% (ordinary rate) |
| Saturday | 150% of ordinary rate |
| Sunday | 200% of ordinary rate |
| Public Holiday | 250% of ordinary rate |
| Overtime — first 2 hours | 150% of ordinary rate |
| Overtime — beyond 2 hours | 200% of ordinary rate |

These percentages apply to the worker's base rate. For casual workers, the casual loading of 25% is included in the base before penalty rates are applied.

## Overtime Triggers

Overtime applies when a worker exceeds:
- 38 hours in a week (for full-time workers), or
- Their agreed ordinary hours for the week (for part-time workers), or
- Their agreed ordinary hours in a single day

**Important:** For part-time workers, hours beyond their agreed hours but within 38 hours per week may still attract overtime rates depending on the roster structure. Check the Award carefully.

## Worked Example — Sunday Shift

A casual Level 3 worker does a 10-hour Sunday shift.

- Base rate Level 3: $27.10/hr
- Casual loading 25%: $27.10 × 1.25 = $33.88/hr
- Sunday penalty 200%: $33.88 × 2.0 = **$67.76/hr**
- Total for 10 hours: **$677.60**

This is significantly higher than a weekday rate of $33.88/hr ($338.80 for the same shift). Getting rostering right — spreading shifts across the week — directly impacts your labour costs.

## Broken Shift Allowance

If a worker's shift is split by an unpaid break of 2 hours or more (a broken shift), they are entitled to a broken shift allowance of approximately **$24.09 per broken shift**, in addition to their ordinary pay for the hours worked.

Many in-home care rosters involve morning and afternoon shifts for the same worker on the same day with a long break in between. Every one of those arrangements triggers the broken shift allowance.

## Sleepover Shifts

Sleepover shifts (where a worker sleeps at a client's premises and is available if needed) attract a flat sleepover allowance of approximately **$51.27 per sleepover**. If the worker is woken and required to work during the sleepover period, those active hours are paid at the applicable ordinary or penalty rate on top of the allowance.

## On-Call Allowance

When a worker is required to be on call (available to be called in but not at the workplace), they are entitled to an on-call allowance. The rate depends on how restricted the worker's movements are during the on-call period. Workers who are heavily restricted (must stay near the workplace, cannot consume alcohol, must respond within 30 minutes) attract a higher allowance than those with minimal restrictions.

## Public Holidays

Workers who work on a public holiday receive 250% of their ordinary rate. Workers who are rostered but not required to work on a public holiday must still be paid their ordinary rate for that day (for full-time and part-time workers).

The specific public holidays that apply depend on the state or territory where the work is performed.

## How SupportIQ Calculates Penalties

When a shift is logged in SupportIQ, the system checks:
1. The day of the week
2. The start and end time
3. Whether the shift crosses midnight into a different penalty rate period
4. Whether it is a public holiday for the relevant state
5. Whether overtime has been triggered for the week

All applicable penalties are calculated and applied automatically. The payslip shows each component separately so workers and managers can see exactly how the total was reached.
