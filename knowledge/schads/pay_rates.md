# SCHADS Award — Pay Rates

## Base Hourly Rates by Classification Level

The following rates are approximate 2025 figures for full-time and part-time employees under the Social and Community Services stream of the SCHADS Award. Rates are reviewed annually by the Fair Work Commission, typically taking effect in July each year. Always verify current rates at fairwork.gov.au.

| Level | Approximate Hourly Rate (2025) | Typical Worker |
|-------|-------------------------------|----------------|
| Level 1 | $24.07 | Entry level, no qualifications, direct supervision |
| Level 2 | $25.50 | Some experience, basic qualifications |
| Level 3 | $27.10 | Certificate III/IV, works with limited supervision |
| Level 4 | $28.80 | Certificate IV, experienced, some complexity |
| Level 5 | $31.20 | Diploma qualified or equivalent experience |
| Level 6 | $33.90 | Senior practitioner, may supervise others |
| Level 7 | $36.80 | Degree qualified, specialist or team leader |
| Level 8 | $39.50 | Senior specialist, management responsibilities |

## Casual Loading

Casual employees receive an additional 25% loading on top of the base hourly rate. This loading compensates for the lack of paid leave entitlements.

**Example:** A casual Level 3 worker earns $27.10 × 1.25 = **$33.88 per hour** (before any penalty rates).

Many NDIS providers roster mostly casual workers for flexibility. The casual loading is non-negotiable and must be applied on every hour worked, including hours that also attract penalty rates.

## Full-Time vs Part-Time vs Casual

**Full-time:** 38 hours per week, entitled to all leave entitlements, no casual loading.

**Part-time:** Agreed regular hours less than 38 per week, entitled to leave on a pro-rata basis, no casual loading, subject to minimum engagement of 2 hours per shift.

**Casual:** No guaranteed hours, no leave entitlements, 25% casual loading on all hours, minimum engagement of 2 hours per shift.

## Junior Rates

Workers under 21 may be paid junior rates as a percentage of the adult rate:
- Under 17: 50% of adult rate
- 17 years: 60%
- 18 years: 70%
- 19 years: 80%
- 20 years: 90%

Junior rates do not apply if the worker holds a Certificate III or higher relevant to their role — in that case, the full adult rate applies.

## First Aid Allowance

Workers who hold a current first aid certificate AND are required by their employer to use that certificate in their role are entitled to approximately $15.03 per week as a first aid allowance. This is in addition to their ordinary pay.

## Annual Rate Review

Every year the Fair Work Commission reviews SCHADS rates as part of the Annual Wage Review. Increases typically take effect from the first full pay period on or after 1 July. Providers must update their payroll systems immediately — continuing to pay old rates after the effective date creates an underpayment liability.

## Common Mistakes

- **Wrong classification:** Classifying a Certificate III worker as Level 1 because the role description sounds basic. Check qualifications and responsibilities against the Award classifications carefully.
- **Forgetting casual loading:** Applying penalty rates but forgetting to include the base casual loading underneath them.
- **Using flat rates:** Some providers set a single flat rate above the Award minimum and assume that covers everything. It does not — penalty rates must be applied on top, not absorbed into a flat rate, unless a formal pay averaging arrangement is in place.
